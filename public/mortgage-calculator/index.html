What Is PITI?

PITI stands for Principal, Interest, Taxes, and Insurance — the four components that make up a typical monthly mortgage payment:

  • Principal — the portion of each payment that reduces your loan balance
  • Interest — the cost of borrowing, calculated on your remaining balance
  • Taxes — property taxes, usually collected monthly and held in escrow
  • Insurance — homeowners insurance, also typically escrowed

Most lenders require escrow for taxes and insurance when your down payment is less than 20%.

30-Year vs. 15-Year Mortgage

A 30-year mortgage offers lower monthly payments and is the most popular choice. A 15-year mortgage has higher payments but you’ll pay significantly less in total interest — often saving six figures on a typical home loan.

Example on a $320,000 loan at 7%:

  • 30-year: ~$2,129/month — $446,450 total interest
  • 15-year: ~$2,877/month — $197,830 total interest
  • Savings with 15-year: ~$248,600 in interest

What Affects Your Mortgage Rate?

  • Credit score — higher scores qualify for better rates
  • Loan-to-value ratio — more down payment = lower rate
  • Loan type — conventional, FHA, VA, and USDA loans all carry different rates
  • Market conditions — the Federal Reserve’s rate decisions ripple into mortgage markets
  • Loan term — shorter terms usually carry lower rates
